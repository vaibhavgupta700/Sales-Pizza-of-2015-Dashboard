# Sales-Pizza-of-2015-Dashboard
shows a dashboard displaying pizza sales analytics for 2015, split into four visual components:

1. Bar chart – “SALES PIZZAS OF 2015 DESHBORAD” (Date Months)
Description: A monthly sales trend of pizza units sold across the year, with individual bars for each month showing quantities (e.g., Jan = 162, Feb = 165, …).
Purpose: Track month‑to‑month sales performance to spot peaks, dips, and seasonality.

2. 3D block chart – “Monthly Sum Of Pizzas”
Description: Aggregated total pizzas sold in a specific month (Jan = 4232 shown in the block).
Purpose: Give an at‑a‑glance view of the overall monthly volume for high‑level comparison.

3. Line graph – “Sum of Pizzas” (daily/weekday)
Description: Cumulative pizza sales across weekdays (Monday‑Saturday) with a smooth trend line showing daily accumulation (e.g., Monday = 444, Friday = 760).
Purpose: Analyze weekday performance and identify which days drive the most sales.

4. Pie chart – “Week Sum Of Pizza”
Description: Distribution of total weekly sales by day (Saturday = 716, Sunday = 489, …).
Purpose: Visualise the share of sales among weekdays to focus marketing on high‑contributing days.

Technology used & business problems addressed- Technology: The dashboard is likely built with a BI tool (e.g., Power BI, Tableau) or Excel, using data visualisation charts to transform raw sales data into interactive insights.
- Business problems / goals:
    - Understand sales fluctuations and seasonality to forecast demand.
    - Identify peak sales periods for inventory & staffing optimisation.
    - Measure overall performance and set monthly/weekly targets.
    - Guide promotional strategies toward low‑performing days/months.

Business impact & insights- Insights:
    - The month with highest sales can guide production planning.
    - Weekday trends show Friday & Saturday drive the bulk of sales → opportunity for weekend specials.
    - Comparing monthly totals helps detect promotional effectiveness or external market changes.
- Impact: Better inventory management, targeted marketing, optimized staffing, and improved revenue forecasting leading to increased profitability.
